The Argument

The Mediterranean diet
is not a cuisine.
It is a structural logic.

"The communities of Delfshaven, Molenbeek and Wilhelmsburg do not need to be taught the Mediterranean diet. They already practice it. Policy needs to recognise it."

Each study in this series makes a different argument — tailored to the specific gap between what a city already has and what it still lacks. Rotterdam needs a framework. Brussels needs to name the one it has already written. Hamburg needs to connect its world-class health science to its food governance bodies.

What all three share: the Mediterranean dietary pattern — legumes, seasonal vegetables, whole grains, olive oil, shared meals, minimal processing — is the scientifically validated, culturally translatable and economically accessible dietary framework that Northern European cities are trying to build. And in every city we have studied, significant communities already embody it.

These studies are free to download. They are also direct proposals to the cities they describe. If you work for one of them, or for a city we have not yet studied — we want to talk.

Methodology

Field knowledge
meets institutional
science.

Every Solco study is grounded in primary public data and validated against the peer-reviewed literature that European funding bodies require.

But the analytical framework comes from the field. From fifteen years of research in Puglia, Greece, Turkey, France, Spain, the Balkans and Northern Europe.

This is what distinguishes a Solco study. It brings the knowledge of what the system looks like when it is alive — and applies that knowledge to the question of how a northern European city can make that logic available to its own population.

City Selection Criteria
Cultural diversity
Significant Mediterranean-heritage or structurally aligned food communities already present in the city's most food-vulnerable districts.
Policy gap
An identifiable, specific absence — a missing framework, an unnamed strategy, an unconnected infrastructure — that the Mediterranean diet can fill.
Institutional readiness
A food council, a municipal food strategy, a public health infrastructure, or a research institution that can act on the study's recommendations.
EU funding alignment
Recommendations designed to be 65–80% fundable through Erasmus+, ESF+, ERDF, Horizon Europe, or national foundation budgets.
